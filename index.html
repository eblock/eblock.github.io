Material Collection

All the objects for a fictional materials archive. The collection celebrates materiality and investigates hoarding, preservation, and conservationism in relation to the perceived worth of both craft objects and natural artifacts.

Bookbinding. 4,5.2015

Archival Samples

Casting experiments in jesmonite that yielded rough-hewn stone samples, collected together here with a number of analogue materials: granite, board, bone, and the ossified skin of a stingray.

Casting. 2-5.2015

Field Notes

Book sketches turned field notebooks. The utilitarian "notebook" is challenged by strange forms that seem to prevent its use. This book micro-collection speaks to infinite regress and embeddedness; manufactured tools reminiscent of geologic rarities.

Bookbinding. 4.2015

Rendered Artifacts

Series of artifacts investigating collection, composition, and assortment. Arbitrary in isolation, artifacts gain value when contextualized, their uniqueness making them seemingly precious. Set of 20 bizarre forms.

Rendering. 5.2015

Landscape Objects

Graphic experiment in 3D texture mapping and collage. The landscape-like surface of a modeled bowl is painted with images of natural environments to part-manually and part-digitally create a chaotic composition.

Collage, Rendering. 4,5.2015

YTB Swap Cards

Splatter painted artist trading cards produced in bulk for Mica and RISD's collaborative YTB Zine. The cards were commissioned for the zine's 8th issue, themed swap, and were made by layering paint, colored jesmonite, and iridescent finishes.

Batch Production. 3.2015

Fossil Watches

Field watch designs for Fossil exploring how a single model can be transformed by altering its color, texture, and materiality. The watch has an elastic wristband that can be worn or alternatively attached to a keychain, carabiner, belt, or other equipment.

3D Modeling. 4.2015

Pendant Rendering

A quick, simple 3D model and sketch rending of a glass pendant lamp. This project is part of small family of lamp renderings I've done and represents an ongoing study of light in digital product renders.

Rendering. 3.2015

Primitive Bowls

A collection of roughly sculpted bowl models rendered in matte tan and in a range of iridescent metals. This series grew out of my love of geology and the unique forms of rocks and stones; it began as an exploration of digital clay processing.

3D Sculpting. 3.2015

Chrysopoeia Evolve 1

Duality—possessing and expressing two states. Like the concept of the homunculus, the tiny life form born of the primordial ooze, chrysopoeia refers to a generative process, conceived by the merging and mutual metamorphosis of disparate ideas.

Writing, Photography. 6.2014

Concept UAV

Speculative prototype and design blueprint for a synthetic-biological, unmanned aerial vehicle. This piece imagines the UAV of a highly technologically advanced future, where materials to produce scientific drones can be grown by organisms in space.

3D Modeling. 5.2014

iGEM Rebrand

This rebrand uses fragments of interesting type to creata a visual allegory for a creative collection: the iGEM registry. iGEM is collaborative, fun, and original—I generated a new identity to celebrate iGEM's uniqueness.

Branding. 6.2014

Biomaterial Production

As a member of the 2014 Stanford–Brown–Spelman iGEM team, I experimented with both synthetic-biological and common industrial biomaterials. I explored shaping starch plastics, cellulose-based bioplastics, and other natural polymer materials.

Biology, Casting. 7,8.2014

Biological UAV

For the 2014 Genetically Engineered Machines Competition, I collaborated with Ecovative Design to grow parts for a biodegradable UAV. With the biodegradable parts, I then constructed a functional bio-drone.

Prototyping. 9.2014
