Material Collection

All the objects for a fictional materials archive. The collection celebrates materiality and investigates hoarding, preservation, and conservationism in relation to the perceived worth of both craft objects and natural artifacts.

Bookbinding. 4,5.2015

Archival Samples

Casting experiments in jesmonite that yielded rough-hewn stone samples, collected together here with a number of analogue materials: granite, board, bone, and the ossified skin of a stingray.

Casting. 2-5.2015

Field Notes

Book sketches turned field notebooks. The utilitarian "notebook" is challenged by strange forms that seem to prevent their use. Still, this book micro-collection speaks to infinite regress and embeddedness; manufactured tools reminiscent of geologic rarities.

Bookbinding. 4.2015

Rendered Artifacts

Series of artifacts investigating collection, composition, and assortment. Arbitrary in isolation, artifacts gain value when contextualized, their uniqueneness making them seemingly precious.

Rendering. 5.2015

Landscape Objects

Graphic experiment in 3D texture mapping and collage. The landscape-like surface of a modeled bowl is painted with images of natural environments.

Collage, Rendering. 4,5.2015

YTB Swap Cards

Primitive Bowls

A collection of roughly sculpted bowl models rendered in matte tan and in a range of iridescent metals. This series began as an exploration of digital clay processing.

3D Sculpting. 3.2015

Chrysopoeia Evolve 1

Duality—possessing and expressing two states. Much like the concept of the homunculus, the tiny life form born of the primordial ooze, chrysopoeia refers to a generative process, one that is conceived by the merging and mutual metamorphosis of disparate ideas.

Writing, Photography. 6.2014

Concept UAV

Speculative prototype and initial design blueprint for a synthetic-biological, unmanned aerial vehicle. This piece imagines the UAV of a highly technologically advanced future, where materials to produce scientific drones can be grown by organisms in space.

3D Modeling. 5.2014

iGEM Rebrand

This rebrand uses fragments of interesting type to creata a visual allegory for a creative collection: the iGEM registry. iGEM is collaborative, fun, and original—I generated a new identity to celebrate iGEM's uniqueness.

Branding. 6.2014

Biomaterial Production

As a member of the 2014 Stanford–Brown–Spelman iGEM team, my team and I experimented with both synthetic-biological and common industrial biomaterials. We explored shaping starch plastics, cellulose-based bioplastics, and other natural polymer materials.

Biology, Casting. 7,8.2014

Biological UAV

For the 2014 Genetically Engineered Machines Competition, I collaborated with Ecovative Design to grow parts for a biodegradable UAV. With the biodegradable parts, I then constructed a functional bio-drone.

Prototyping. 9.2014
